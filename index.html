How we write a recipe

Precision first,
so you can improvise second.

01

Weighed, not scooped

Every recipe is written in grams first, with cups alongside for convenience. A cup of flour can vary by 20% depending on how it's packed — that's the difference between a biscuit and a brick.

02

Tested on ordinary ovens

No convection assumptions, no professional deck ovens. If a recipe calls for 190°C, we baked it in a normal home oven with a hot spot and a squeaky door to prove it.

03

Told to you straight

"Bake until golden" means nothing. We tell you the minute, the smell, and the sound to listen for, so you're never guessing at the last five minutes.
