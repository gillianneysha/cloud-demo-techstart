Ingredients

  • 227gunsalted butter
  • 300glight brown sugar
  • 100ggranulated sugar
  • 2large eggs, room temp
  • 2 tspvanilla extract
  • 375gall-purpose flour
  • 1 tspbaking soda
  • 1¼ tspfine salt
  • 280gdark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tspflaky salt, to finish

Method

  1. Brown the butter in a light-colored pan over medium heat, swirling often, until it smells nutty and the solids turn amber, about 6 minutes. Pour into a bowl to cool for 15 minutes.
  2. Whisk the browned butter with both sugars until glossy. Add eggs and vanilla; whisk until pale and slightly thickened, about 1 minute.
  3. Fold in flour, baking soda and salt just until no dry streaks remain. Fold in the chocolate.
  4. Rest the dough, covered, in the fridge for at least 24 hours — this is what deepens the flavor. It'll keep up to 3 days.
  5. Scoop 65g balls onto a lined tray, spaced well apart. Sprinkle with flaky salt.
  6. Bake at 190°C for 11–13 minutes, until the edges are set but the centers still look slightly underdone. Cool on the tray for 10 minutes before moving.
Note: Skipping the rest still works, but the flavor will taste flatter and the cookies will spread more.

Ingredients

  • 100gactive sourdough starter
  • 375gwater, room temp
  • 500gbread flour
  • 10gfine sea salt
  • —rice flour, for the banneton

Method

  1. Mix starter and water until dissolved, then add flour. Mix until no dry patches remain and rest 30 minutes (autolyse).
  2. Sprinkle in the salt and squeeze it through the dough. Rest 30 minutes.
  3. Bulk ferment 4–5 hours at room temperature, performing a set of stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours. The dough should grow by about half and feel airy.
  4. Shape into a tight round, place seam-up in a floured banneton, cover, and refrigerate 12–18 hours.
  5. Heat a Dutch oven at 240°C for 45 minutes. Turn the loaf out onto parchment, score with one confident slash, and lower into the pot.
  6. Bake covered for 20 minutes, then uncovered for 22–25 minutes more, until deeply browned. Cool at least 1 hour before slicing.
Note: No banneton? A flour-dusted bowl lined with a clean tea towel works just as well.

Ingredients

  • 500gstore-bought or homemade laminated dough
  • 120gunsalted butter, softened
  • 150gbrown sugar
  • 2 tbspground cinnamon
  • 120ggranulated sugar, for the pans
  • 1egg, beaten, for wash

Method

  1. Roll the dough into a rectangle roughly 40 × 60cm. Spread evenly with softened butter.
  2. Mix brown sugar and cinnamon; scatter over the buttered dough in an even layer.
  3. Roll tightly from the long edge into a log. Slice into 12 rounds and place cut-side up into sugar-coated muffin tins.
  4. Cover and proof in a warm spot for 1½–2 hours, until visibly puffed.
  5. Brush with egg wash and bake at 200°C for 22–25 minutes, until deeply golden.
  6. Invert onto a tray immediately while hot, so the caramelized sugar releases from the tin.
Note: Work fast when rolling — the butter should stay cold and streaky, not melt into the dough.

Ingredients

  • 280gall-purpose flour
  • 1½ tspbaking powder
  • ½ tspbaking soda
  • ¾ tspfine salt
  • 3large eggs
  • 300ggranulated sugar
  • 2 tbsplemon zest (about 3 lemons)
  • 240mlextra-virgin olive oil
  • 240mlwhole milk
  • 3 tbspfresh lemon juice

Method

  1. Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt together; set aside.
  2. Rub lemon zest into the sugar with your fingertips until fragrant. Whisk in eggs until pale and thick, about 2 minutes.
  3. Slowly whisk in the olive oil, then the milk and lemon juice.
  4. Fold the dry ingredients into the wet in three additions, mixing just until combined.
  5. Pour into a greased 23cm round pan and bake at 175°C for 45–50 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean.
  6. Cool in the pan 15 minutes, then turn out onto a rack to cool fully before slicing.
Note: Use a fruity, mild olive oil — a bitter or peppery one will fight the lemon.

Ingredients

  • 375gall-purpose flour
  • 2 tbspbaking powder
  • 1 tspfine salt
  • 1 tbspgranulated sugar
  • 170gcold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 280mlcold buttermilk
  • 2 tbspmelted butter, to finish

Method

  1. Whisk flour, baking powder, salt and sugar together. Scatter in cold butter and rub or cut it in until pea-sized pieces remain.
  2. Pour in buttermilk and stir with a fork just until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Turn out onto a floured surface. Pat into a rectangle, fold into thirds like a letter, and repeat twice more to build layers.
  4. Pat to 2.5cm thick and cut rounds with a sharp cutter, pressing straight down without twisting.
  5. Place close together on a tray so they rise upward. Bake at 220°C for 14–16 minutes, until tall and golden.
  6. Brush with melted butter as soon as they come out of the oven.
Note: Keep everything cold — chill the flour and cut the butter straight from the fridge for the flakiest layers.

Ingredients

  • 250mlwhole milk, warm
  • 7ginstant yeast
  • 500gall-purpose flour
  • 70ggranulated sugar
  • 1½ tspground cardamom
  • ½ tspfine salt
  • 75gunsalted butter, softened
  • 100gbutter, softened, for filling
  • 80gsugar, for filling
  • 1½ tspcardamom, for filling
  • —pearl sugar, to finish

Method

  1. Warm milk to lukewarm and stir in yeast. Combine with flour, sugar, cardamom and salt; mix to a shaggy dough.
  2. Knead in the softened butter in pieces until the dough is smooth and elastic, about 8 minutes. Cover and proof 1 hour, until doubled.
  3. Mix the filling butter with filling sugar and cardamom into a spreadable paste.
  4. Roll the dough into a large rectangle and spread with the filling. Fold into thirds, then slice into strips and twist each into a knot.
  5. Place knots on trays, cover, and proof 45 minutes until puffy. Brush with milk and press tops into pearl sugar.
  6. Bake at 210°C for 10–12 minutes, until deep golden brown.
Note: Freshly ground cardamom pods make a noticeable difference over pre-ground — worth the extra step.
