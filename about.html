Who's behind the counter

It started with one loaf that wouldn't rise.

Second Rise began as a single failing sourdough starter and a notebook of what went wrong. Six years later it's 86 recipes, all still tested in the same small kitchen.

What we hold onto

Three rules we don't break

Time is an ingredient

Rests, proofs and chills aren't padding — they're where flavor and structure actually form. We never shortcut them just to make a recipe look faster.

One good version beats ten mediocre ones

We'd rather publish six recipes we'd defend at a dinner party than sixty we're unsure of. Every one on this site has earned its place.

Ordinary kitchens, ordinary tools

No stand mixer requirements, no specialty flours you can't find. If a recipe needs something unusual, we tell you upfront and offer a substitute.

Before you write in

Questions we get a lot

Can I substitute all-purpose flour for bread flour?

In most cookie and cake recipes, yes, with no real difference. In the sourdough and cardamom bun recipes, it will work but the crumb will be a little less chewy — bread flour's higher protein is what builds that structure.

Do you test with US or metric measurements?

Every recipe is developed and tested in grams first. Cup measurements are provided as a convenience, but for anything with more than three or four ingredients, we'd genuinely recommend a kitchen scale.

Can I email you a recipe request?

Always. We keep a running list and work through it slowly, in the order things get requested most. Use the newsletter signup below and reply to any issue — it comes straight to us.

Why don't you use volume-only measurements?

A cup of flour can weigh anywhere from 120g to 150g depending on how it's scooped. That swing is often the actual difference between a recipe working and not — so we measure the way that removes the guesswork.
